The Inevitable Growth Of Technology

Written by Omendra Kumar Upadhyay | Published on July 2, 2025

The evolution of the human species, from a single cell to the intelligent beings we are today, spans over six million years. Central to this journey is the development of technology, a tool created by humans to solve problems and advance civilization. However, this raises an important question: Does every technological advancement genuinely solve problems or improve our lives?

One stark example of technology's darker side is the MK-Ultra experiment, conducted by the CIA from 1953 to 1964. This chapter in scientific history illustrates the extreme lengths to which some organizations will go to achieve dominance in espionage and intelligence. The goal of MK-Ultra was to explore methods of mind control, driven by the desire to manipulate the most intelligent species on Earth.

Among its many subprojects, Subproject 68, led by Dr. Ewen Cameron at the Allen Memorial Institute in Canada, stands out. Funded by a CIA front organization, this experiment involved administering high doses of LSD and other drugs to patients who were often misdiagnosed with mental disorders. For instance, Robert Logey, an 18-year-old seeking treatment for leg pain, was subjected to electric shocks and drug treatments that had nothing to do with his initial complaint. Such actions sparked widespread criticism for violating research ethics.

But consider this: What if the experiment had been successful? What if researchers had discovered a method to control human behavior? The implications would have been staggering, potentially equipping the CIA with unprecedented power in espionage while offering psychiatrists new treatment options for mental health.

Similarly, when Dr. Oppenheimer and his team developed the world's first atomic bomb, fears emerged that it could lead to catastrophic consequences, even igniting the atmosphere. Today, however, nuclear technology accounts for about 10% of global electricity production, showcasing its dual nature as both a source of fear and a means of energy generation.

It's important to recognize that defense technology often advances at least 20 years ahead of commercial technology. The classified innovations of today may become the solutions to tomorrow's challenges.

Ultimately, technological progress is inevitable, and civilization must continue to evolve. While it's crucial to remain vigilant about the potential misuse of new technologies, we cannot allow fear to halt our progress. The journey of human innovation began long ago, and it cannot be stifled. Embracing this evolution is essential to staying in sync with the changing world and addressing the challenges we face.
